# Topological/discrete action-sector probe — research plan

Step (1) of the closure programme identified in the BAM
effective-action scaffold (PR #49). The scaffold found five mismatch
terms blocking a single covariant action; two of them — B1 (closure
quantum) and B2 (antipodal Z₂) — were flagged as the cleanest to
promote from *imposed constraints* to *terms in a topological action*.
This probe tests that promotion.

## The two barriers

  - **B1 — closure quantum** `∮A = 2πn` (`action_base = 2π`). In the
    scaffold this is a Wilson-loop / holonomy constraint imposed by
    hand, not a local Lagrangian density.

  - **B2 — antipodal Z₂** `σ: p → −p` (`T = iσ_y`, `T² = −I`). In the
    scaffold this is a discrete identification imposed by hand, not a
    symmetry of the smooth action.

## The promotion: a topological/discrete sector

The claim under test: **B1 and B2 are not two unrelated imposed
constraints — they are both data of a single topological/discrete
sector**, namely the antipodal quotient

```
RP³ = S³ / Z₂        (Z₂ generated by σ: p → −p)
```

equipped with its **non-trivial spin structure** and a **winding
θ-term**. Concretely:

  1. **B2 → quotient + spin structure.** The antipodal map σ is an
     involution (`σ² = id`); the quotient `S³/Z₂ = RP³` makes the
     identification part of the manifold. `RP³` has exactly two spin
     structures (`H¹(RP³, Z₂) = Z₂`); the spinor transport
     `T = iσ_y` with `T² = −I` (4π periodicity) **selects the
     non-trivial one**. The discrete Z₂ becomes `π₁(RP³) = Z₂`, a
     topological invariant, not an imposed symmetry.

  2. **B1 → winding θ-term.** The closure quantum is the winding
     number of the phase map `S¹ → U(1)` around the closed orbit:
     `∮ dφ = 2π·n`. This is a topological total-derivative term
     `S_top = θ · n` with `θ = 2π` — the "one full turn" of phase,
     metric-independent (2π radians, not 2πR length). Single-valuedness
     of the wave forces integer `n`; the term *is* the closure
     quantum, no longer imposed.

  3. **The double cover ties them together.** A great circle on `S³`
     (length 2π = the closure quantum) projects to a non-contractible
     loop on `RP³` (length π) traversed **twice**. So the closure
     quantum (B1, one full great-circle winding) is exactly a double
     traversal of the `π₁(RP³) = Z₂` generator (B2). The two barriers
     are two faces of the same `S³ → RP³` double-cover structure.

## Predictions

### P1. Antipodal involution generates Z₂

`σ² = id` exactly; the quotient `S³/Z₂ = RP³` has `π₁ = Z₂`.

### P2. Double-cover relates closure quantum to the Z₂ generator

A great circle on `S³` (length 2π = closure quantum) = double traversal
of the non-contractible `RP³` loop (length π). The winding/closure
structure is the double cover.

### P3. `T² = −I` is the non-trivial spin structure on RP³

`T = iσ_y`, `T² = −I`; this is one of the two spin structures on RP³,
the one with antiperiodic spinors (4π periodicity). It is topological
data, not an imposed sign.

### P4. Winding θ-term is variationally consistent

The winding term `S_top = 2π·n` is a topological total derivative; it
does not modify the local Euler–Lagrange equations (metric- and
path-detail-independent). It can be added to the smooth action without
conflict.

### P5. K and Q follow from the topological sector + smooth dynamics

With B1 (winding θ-term) and B2 (RP³ quotient + spin structure) now
*action data*, the throat-action results `K(x) = 2x/(1+x)` and
`Q(x, c)` (PRs #39–#41) follow from the stationary action **without
separately imposing** the closure quantum or the antipodal symmetry.

## Tests

  T1. **Antipodal involution → Z₂** (P1): `σ² = id` (antipode4);
      quotient structure.
  T2. **Double cover S³ → RP³** (P2): great circle (2π) = double
      traversal of RP³ loop (π); geodesic lengths via `geo4`.
  T3. **π₁(RP³) = Z₂** (P2): non-contractible loop squared lifts to a
      closed (contractible) S³ path, so `[γ]² = 1`.
  T4. **Spin structure T² = −I** (P3): `derive_throat_transport`,
      `verify_transport_properties`; the non-trivial spin structure.
  T5. **Winding θ-term** (P1, P4): `∮dφ = 2πn` winding number;
      topological total derivative; metric-independent.
  T6. **Variational consistency** (P4): the winding term does not
      change the local EOM (it is a boundary/topological term).
  T7. **K and Q from the topological sector** (P5): recompute
      `K = 2x/(1+x)` (closure quantum from winding) and `Q` (antipodal
      split from the RP³ Z₂), verify `F² = K²·Q`, now sourcing the
      closure quantum and antipodal symmetry from the topological
      sector rather than imposing them.
  T8. **Promotion assessment / barrier-count reduction**: B1 and B2
      promoted to a topological/discrete action sector; scaffold
      barriers reduced from 5 to 3 (B3, B4, B5 remain).

## Verdict structure

  - **PROMOTION_SUCCEEDS**: B1 → winding θ-term and B2 → RP³ quotient
    + non-trivial spin structure are both genuine topological/discrete
    action data. The closure quantum and antipodal Z₂ are no longer
    imposed; the scaffold barrier count drops from 5 to 3. The
    topological sector is `RP³ + non-trivial spin structure + 2π
    winding θ-term`.

  - **PROMOTION_PARTIAL**: one of B1/B2 promotes cleanly but the other
    retains an imposed residual.

  - **PROMOTION_FAILS**: the topological structure does not reproduce
    the closure quantum or antipodal symmetry used by the throat
    action.

## What this leaves open (the residual 3 barriers)

  - **B3 — hard-wall boundary conditions.** The Dirichlet condition at
    the throat from `T² = −I` is consistent with the non-trivial spin
    structure identified here, but deriving the boundary condition
    itself from the bulk action is a separate step.
  - **B4 — dimensional bridge.** The 2π winding is metric-independent
    (topological), but the *physical* great-circle length 2πR and the
    mass scale still need the `m_e` anchor.
  - **B5 — 5D → 4D reduction.** Unaffected by this promotion; the
    radial reduction producing F² remains unconstructed.
